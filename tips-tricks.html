Tips & Tricks

To help your condition

Always talk about your diagnosis and/or condition to a professional first!

General

- Always seek professional help (doctor, therapist and psychiatrist)

- Connect with people that share your diagnosis and/or condition and share your experiences.

- Don't be ashamed of your condition (Love yourself).

- Confide your thoughts and problems to someone you trust (friends and/or family).

ASD Tips:

- When your emotions start to feel overwhelming take a deep breath, relax, and do whatever calms you down.

- In conversations deeply observe what is happening as an event and try to define the connection between what is happening and what you're feeling.

- Be physically active.

- Learn to work with your strengths.


Source: www.ineffableliving.com

ADHD Tips:

- Check with your doctor if a medication subscribtion makes sense.

- Use journals/planners that are made for ADHD (or a selfmade journal/planner that fits your needs).

- Start small when doing projects or cleaning to enter hyperfocus.

- Do sports.

Source: www.talkwithfrida.com

Anxiety Tips:

- Practice focused deep breathing.

- Write down your thoughts.

- Learn to manage your triggers.

- Ask your doctor about medication.

- Question your thought pattern.

Source: www.healthline.com

Depression Tips:

- Get enough sleep

- Try to eat healthy.

- Have a routine.

- Do sports.

- Reduce your stress.

- Learn how to focus your negative thoughts.

Source: www.verywellmind.com

PTSD Tips:

- Learn how to soothe yourself.

- Distract yourself when coping with uncomfortable emotions.

- Carry an object that reminds you of present.

- Keep a diary.

Source: www.verywellmind.com

Schizophrenia Tips:

- find support groups.

- Consider therapy/treatment.

- establish a support system (people you feel safe with)

- Talk to your doctor about medication

Source: www.psychcentral.com

Eating Disorder Tips:

- Eat at set times.

- Keep a grattitude journal.

- Try empowerment and selflove trainings (Self-Care).

- Know your triggers.

- Consider therapy/treatment.

Source: www.verywellmind.com

Never forget that you aren't alone
